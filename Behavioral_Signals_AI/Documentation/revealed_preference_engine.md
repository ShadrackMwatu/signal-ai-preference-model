# Revealed Preference Engine

The revealed preference engine interprets aggregate behavioral traces as economic signals. It does not infer what any person did. It infers what public aggregate behavior may reveal about market pressure, demand, need, substitution, and opportunity.

## Inputs

- Search trend signals such as `maize flour prices`, `jobs in Nairobi`, `cheap smartphones`, or `hospital near me`.
- Aggregate social trends such as public concern, hashtags, national conversation, and engagement velocity.
- Public news/context signals related to prices, labour pressure, food insecurity, service demand, and supply disruptions.

## Inferences

The engine produces:

- revealed interest;
- revealed need;
- revealed pressure;
- revealed aggregate demand;
- substitution effects;
- emerging consumption shifts.

These outputs feed demand intelligence, opportunity intelligence, recommendations, and forecasting.

## Privacy Boundary

The engine uses only aggregate public indicators and privacy-safe provider records. It does not process individual profiles, raw private content, or personal identifiers.
