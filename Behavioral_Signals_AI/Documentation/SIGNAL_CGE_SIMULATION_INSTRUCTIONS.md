# Signal CGE Simulation Instructions

## Reduce Import Tariffs

Prompt: `reduce import tariffs on cmach by 10%`

- Policy instrument: import tariff.
- Target account: `cmach`.
- Shock direction: decrease.
- Expected transmission channel: lower tariff wedge, lower import price pressure, higher import demand, lower tariff revenue, pressure on domestic substitutes, and uncertain trade-balance effect.
- Expected outputs: import price, import demand, tariff revenue, household welfare proxy, output, and trade balance.

## Increase VAT

Prompt: `increase VAT by 5%`

- Policy instrument: VAT or indirect tax.
- Target account: broad commodity tax base unless a sector is specified.
- Shock direction: increase.
- Expected transmission channel: higher consumer prices, higher government revenue, lower real purchasing power, and possible lower demand.
- Expected outputs: prices, household welfare proxy, government balance, output, and sector demand.

## Increase Government Spending

Prompt: `increase government spending on health by 10%`

- Policy instrument: government demand.
- Target account: health.
- Shock direction: increase.
- Expected transmission channel: higher public demand, increased linked activity output, fiscal pressure depending on closure, and factor/household income effects.
- Expected outputs: output, factor income, household income, government balance, and welfare proxy.

## Double Care Infrastructure Investment

Prompt: `double investment in care infrastructure`

- Policy instrument: investment shock.
- Target account: care infrastructure.
- Shock direction: increase by 100 percent.
- Expected transmission channel: higher investment demand, factor demand effects, care-service capacity effects, and household welfare effects.
- Expected outputs: investment demand, output, factor income, household income, and gender-care impact where accounts are available.

## Increase Agriculture Productivity

Prompt: `increase productivity in agriculture by 8%`

- Policy instrument: productivity shock.
- Target account: agriculture activity or commodity account.
- Shock direction: increase.
- Expected transmission channel: lower unit costs, output response, price effects, factor demand changes, and real household income effects.
- Expected outputs: output, price, factor income, household welfare proxy, and structural change.
