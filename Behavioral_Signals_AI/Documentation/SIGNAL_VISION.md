# Signal Vision

Last updated: 2026-05-08

Signal is intended to become a Kenyan and African public intelligence platform that connects behavioral evidence, market opportunity, and policy simulation.

## Core Belief

Public decisions and market decisions are often made with delayed evidence. Signal exists to shorten that delay by detecting aggregate patterns early, interpreting them responsibly, and translating them into actionable analysis.

## Strategic Pillars

1. Revealed preference intelligence.
2. Privacy-preserving aggregate analytics.
3. AI-assisted policy and market interpretation.
4. SML/CGE simulation workflows.
5. Adaptive learning from feedback and run history.
6. Lightweight deployment through Gradio and Hugging Face.

## Intended Users

- Policy analysts.
- Economic modelers.
- County and national planning teams.
- Market intelligence teams.
- Researchers.
- Entrepreneurs.
- Development practitioners.

## Long-Term Product Direction

Signal should evolve from a dashboard into an intelligence operating system:

- It listens to aggregate public and market signals.
- It classifies demand and unmet need.
- It explains why signals matter.
- It proposes policy and market actions.
- It validates economic scenarios.
- It learns from mistakes.
